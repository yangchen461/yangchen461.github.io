1. Best Time to Sell Stocks

Given an array $prices$, whose $i$-th element $prices[i]$ represents the price of a given stock on the $i$th day.

You can only choose to buy this stock on a certain day and choose to sell it on a different day in the future. Design an algorithm to calculate the maximum profit you can get.

Return the maximum profit you can get from this transaction. If you cannot get any profit, return $0$.

Analysis

The core equation of “Best time to sell stocks” question is:
$$
maxProfit = prices[i] - minprice
$$
In this question, we only need to use a variable to record the historical lowest price $minprice$, and we can assume that out stock was bought on that day. Then the profit we can get from selling the stock on the $i$th day is $prices[i] - minprice$.

Therefore, we only need to traverse the price array once, record the historical lowest point, and then consider this question every day: If I bought it at the historical lowest point, how much money can I make by selling it today? when all the days are considered, we get the best answer.

  • Time Complexity: $O(n^2)$

  • Space Complexity: $O(1)$

2. Best Time to Sell Stocks II

Question Description

Given an integer array $prices$, where $prices[i]$ represents the price of a stock on day $i$.

Once each day, you can decide whether to buy and/or sell the stock. You can only hold at most one share of the stock at any time. You can also buy and then sell on the same day.

Return the maximum profit you can make.

Analysis

The core equation of “Best time to sell stocks II” question is:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[i][0] = max(dp[i-1][0],dp[i-1][1] + prices[i])\\
dp[i][1] = max(dp[i-1][1],dp[i-1][0] - prices[0])
\end{cases}
$$
In the above equation, we use $’0’$ to represent ‘No stock hold’, and use $’1’$ to represent ‘stock hold’.

Define the state $dp[i][0]$ to represent the maximum profit of holding no stock after the $i$th day of trading, and $dp[i][1]$ to represent the maximum profit of holding a stock after the $i$th day of trading($i$ starts at $0$).

The boundary conditions:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[0][0] = 0 \\
dp[0][1] = - prices[0]
\end{cases}
$$

3. Best Time to Buy and Sell Stocks III

Question Description

Given an array whose $i$th elements is the price of a given stock on the $i$th day.

Design an algorithm to calculate the maximum profit you can make. You can complete at most two trades.

Note: You cannot participate in multiple trades at the same time(you must sell the previous stock before buying it again).

Analysis

The core equation of “Best Time to Buy and Sell Stocks III” is :

$$
\begin{cases}
dp[i][k][0] = max(dp[i-1][k][0],dp[i-1][k][1] + prices[i]) \\
dp[i][k][1] = max(dp[i-1][k][1],dp[i-1][k-1][0] - prices[i])
\end{cases}
$$
In the above equation, we use $’0’$ to represent ‘No stock hold’, and use $’1’$ to represent ‘stock hold’.

Moreover, $dp[i][k][s]$ means: “On the $i$th day, there are at most $k$ transactions and the maximum profit when the holding status is $s(0/1)$”.

The boundary conditions:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[0][k][0] = 0,& \text{k = 0,1,2}\\
dp[0][k][1] = - prices[i],& \text{k = 0,1,2}
\end{cases}
$$
While the question acquire “You can complete at most two trades”, $k$ must satisfy $k < 3$.

Complexity Analysis

  • Time Complexity: $O(n)$
  • Space Complexity: $O(n)$

4. Best Time to Sell Stocks IV

Question Description

Given an integer array $prices$ and an integer $k$, where $prices[i]$ represents the price of a stock on day $i$.

Design an algorithm to calculate the maximum profit you can make. You can complete at most $k$ trades. That is, you can buy at most $k$ times and sell at most $k$ times.

Note: You cannot participate in multiple trades at the same time(you must sell the previous stock before buying it again).

Analysis

The core equation of “Best time to sell stocks IV” question is:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[i][k][0] = max(dp[i-1][k][0],dp[i-1][k][1] + prices[i]) \\
dp[i][k][1] = max(dp[i-1][k][1],dp[i-1][k-1][0] - prices[i])
\end{cases}
$$
In the above equation, we use $’0’$ to represent ‘No stock hold’, and use $’1’$ to represent ‘stock hold’.

Moreover, $dp[i][k][s]$ means: “On the $i$th day, there are at most $k$ transactions and the maximum profit when the holding status is $s(0/1)$”.

The boundary conditions:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[0][k][0] = 0,& \text{k < m+1}\\
dp[0][k][1] = - prices[i],& \text{k < m+1}
\end{cases}
$$
While the question acquire “You can complete at most two trades”, $k$ must satisfy $k < m+1$.

5. Best Time to Buy and Sell Stocks Including Freeze Period

Question Description

Given an integer array $prices$, where $prices[i]$ represents the stock price on the $i$th day.

Design an algorithm to calculate the maximum profit. You can complete as many transactions as possible(buy and sell a stock multiple times) under the following constraints:

After selling a stock, you cannot but it back the next day(i.e. the freezing period is 1 day).

Note: You cannot participate in multiple transactions at the same time(you must sell the previous stock before buying it again).

Analysis

The core equation of “Best time to buy and sell stocks including freeze period” question is:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[i][0] = max(dp[i-1][0],dp[i-1][1] - prices[i]) \\
dp[i][1] = max(dp[i-1][1],dp[i-1][2])\\
dp[i][2] = dp[i][0] + prices[i]
\end{cases}
$$
The boundary conditions:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[0][0] = - prices[i]\\
dp[0][1] = 0 \\
dp[0][2] = 0
\end{cases}
$$
In the above equation, we divide this question into three different conditions.

We use $’0’$ to represent ‘stock hold’, and use $’1’$ to represent ‘no stock hold but in the non-freezing period’ and use $’2’$ to represent ‘no stock hold and in the freezing period’.

Moreover, $dp[i][s]$ means: “On the $i$th day, the maximum profit when the holding status is $s(0/1/2)$”.

6. The Best Time to Buy and Sell Stocks with Commission

Question Description

Given an integer array $prices$, where $prices[i]$ represents the stock prices on the $i$th day; the integer fee represents the commission for trading stocks.

You can complete transactions as many times as you want, but you need to pay a commission for each transaction. If you have already bought a stock, you cannot continue to buy stocks before selling it.

Return the maximum profit.

Note: A transaction here refers to the entire process of buying, holding and selling stocks. You only need to pay a commission once for each transaction.

Analysis

The core equation of “Best time to buy and sell stocks with commission” question is:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[i][0] = max(dp[i-1][0],dp[i-1][1] + prices[i] - fee) \\
dp[i][1] = max(dp[i-1][1],dp[i-1][0] - prices[i])
\end{cases}
$$
The boundary conditions:
$$
\begin{cases}
dp[0][0] = 0\\
dp[0][1] = - prices[i]
\end{cases}
$$

Details

Why is the transaction fee subtracted when ‘selling’ instead of added when ‘buying’?

From the semantics of the transaction:

  • The handling fee is incurred in conjunction with a complete “buy + sell” and is only charged once;
  • It is generally believed that only when selling is the transaction truly completed and the handling fee needs to be paid.
